## The Thesis

Every small business owner in America is being told they need AI. Most of them
are being sold something they don't need by someone who learned about it six
months ago. This book is the antidote: written by someone who actually builds
AI systems, lives in Lancaster, Ohio, and would rather you learn to do it
yourself than hire anyone — including him.

The honest author angle: "I've been building AI systems for years. My prediction
track record is 42% — which means I'm wrong more than I'm right, and I'll tell
you that upfront. What I AM right about is what AI can and can't do for a small
business, because I've tested it."

## PART ONE: THE BULLSHIT DETECTOR

### Chapter 1: The AI Gold Rush Is Coming to Main Street
- 800,000 management consultants in the US; in 5 years there will be that many "AI consultants"
- Most of them know less than you will after reading this book
- The pattern: new technology → hype cycle → snake oil → useful tools → everyone uses it
- We're in the snake oil phase. Here's how to survive it.
- **The 5 Red Flags of AI Snake Oil**:
  1. "AI will 10x your revenue" (no specific mechanism explained)
  2. They can't demo it live with YOUR data
  3. The pricing is based on "value" not on actual costs
  4. They won't explain what's under the hood in plain English
  5. They promise "set it and forget it" (AI always needs oversight)

### Chapter 2: What AI Actually Is (In Plain English)
- Not a brain. Not magic. Pattern matching on text, images, and sound.
- The 4 things AI does well right now:
  1. Generate first drafts of text (emails, posts, descriptions)
  2. Summarize and extract info from large documents
  3. Answer questions about specific content it's been given
  4. Handle routine phone calls and scheduling
- The 4 things AI is terrible at:
  1. Judgment calls that require knowing YOUR customers
  2. Anything where being wrong has serious consequences
  3. Replacing relationships and trust
  4. Working without human oversight ("autopilot" is a myth)
- The cost reality: most useful AI tools are $20-100/month, not $5,000

- Questions to ask before you pay anyone:
  1. "Can you show me this working with a business like mine?"
  2. "What happens when I stop paying you?"
  3. "What does this cost you to run per month?" (they should answer this)
  4. "What's your track record? Show me failures."
  5. "Will I own this, or do I rent it from you?"

- What fair pricing looks like
