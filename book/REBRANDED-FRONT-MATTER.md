## About This Book

This book started as a guide for Lancaster, Ohio. Population 40,000, give or take. Fairfield County seat. A town with a Kroger, a couple of stoplights on Main Street, and a lot of business owners who've been hearing the word "AI" for two years without anyone telling them the truth about it.

I wrote it because nobody else was going to. The big AI companies don't care about Lancaster. The consultants rolling through town care about Lancaster's money, not Lancaster's businesses. And the books on Amazon are written for tech people in San Francisco, not shop owners in Fairfield County.

Then I realized something: Lancaster isn't special. I don't mean that as an insult — I live here. I mean the problems are the same everywhere. The plumber in Lancaster who misses calls after 5pm has the same problem as the plumber in Dubuque, Iowa. The restaurant owner trying to manage social media has the same headache whether she's on Main Street in Lancaster or Main Street in Bennington, Vermont.

So instead of selling this book, I'm giving it away. Open source. CC BY-SA 4.0. That means anyone can take it, change it, and publish their own version for their own town. The only rule is you have to share your version under the same terms, so the next person can do the same thing.

Here's the part that matters: every town that creates their own version makes every other town's version more valuable. That's not a metaphor. It's an SEO network. When "AI for Dubuque" links to "AI for Lancaster" and "AI for Lancaster" links to "AI for Bennington," all three rank higher in their local searches. When someone Googles "AI help for small business Dubuque," they find a version written by someone who actually lives there and knows the local businesses.

The goal was never the book. The goal was the network.

If you're a business owner reading this, everything in this book applies to you. Use it.

If you're someone who wants to create a version for your own town, skip to the last chapter: "Fork This Book." It'll walk you through the whole process.
