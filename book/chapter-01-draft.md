# Chapter 1: The AI Gold Rush Is Coming to Main Street

There are about 800,000 management consultants in the United States right now.
In five years, there will probably be that many people calling themselves "AI
consultants." A good portion of them will be showing up at your door.

Some of them will be genuinely helpful. Most of them will be selling you
something you don't need, built on technology they don't fully understand,
for a problem you might not actually have.

I'm writing this book so you can tell the difference.

## Who I Am and Why You Should Listen (Cautiously)

My name is Leo Guinan. I live in Lancaster, Ohio. I've been building AI systems
for years — not the kind you see in movies, but the practical kind: tools that
process information, make predictions, automate repetitive work.

Here's something most AI consultants won't tell you: my prediction track record
is about 42%. That means I'm wrong more than I'm right. I publish my
predictions before I know the outcomes, and I publish the misses with the same
weight as the hits.

Why would I lead with that? Because it's the most important thing about me.
Anyone who tells you they know exactly what AI will do for your business is
either lying or delusional. The honest answer is: it depends. It depends on
your business, your customers, your workflows, and what you're actually
willing to change.

What I CAN tell you — with high confidence — is what AI does well right now,
what it does poorly, and how to spot the difference between someone who wants
to help you and someone who wants your money.

## The Pattern You've Seen Before

If you've been in business for more than ten years, you've already survived
one of these cycles. Remember when everyone needed a website? Around 2005-2010,
a wave of "web consultants" rolled through every small town in America.

Some of them built good websites for fair prices. A lot of them charged
$10,000 for something that was worth $500. Some of them disappeared after
cashing the check.

The same thing happened with social media marketing around 2015. And "digital
transformation" around 2020. Same pattern every time:

1. New technology emerges that genuinely matters
2. Early adopters figure it out and get real advantages
3. A gold rush of consultants floods the market
4. Most of the consultants know less than they claim
5. Small businesses get burned
6. The technology eventually becomes normal and affordable
7. Everyone uses it, no consultant needed

We're at step 3 right now with AI. The technology is real. The advantages are
real. But the gold rush means you need to be careful about who you trust.

## The Five Red Flags of AI Snake Oil

I want to give you a simple checklist. Tape this to the wall behind your desk.
Any time someone walks into your business or sends you an email about AI
services, run them through these five questions.

### Red Flag #1: They Promise Revenue Multiples Without Specifics

"AI will 10x your revenue!" is not a business proposal. It's a bumper sticker.

If someone tells you AI will dramatically increase your revenue, the very
next word out of your mouth should be: "How, specifically?" And the answer
should involve YOUR business, YOUR numbers, and a mechanism you can understand.

"We'll set up a voice agent that answers your phone 24/7. Based on your
current call volume of 40 calls per week and the fact that you're missing
about 30% of them, we estimate you'll capture an additional 12 calls per week.
At your average ticket of $200, that's roughly $2,400 per month in recovered
revenue."

That's a specific claim you can evaluate. "10x your revenue with AI" is not.

### Red Flag #2: They Can't Demo It Live With Your Data

This is the most important test. If someone is selling you an AI tool or
service, ask them to show it working — right now, with information from your
actual business.

Not a pre-recorded demo. Not a PowerPoint. Not "we'll build it after you sign."
Right now, in front of you, with your data.

If they can't do that, one of two things is true: they don't actually have a
working product, or their product doesn't work well enough to show in an
uncontrolled environment. Either way, keep your wallet closed.

### Red Flag #3: The Pricing Is Based on "Value," Not Costs

"This is worth $50,000 in value to your business" is a classic consulting
trick. It's not wrong — value-based pricing is legitimate — but when the
underlying tool costs $50 per month to run and took 10 hours to set up,
charging $50,000 is not value-based pricing. It's exploitation of your
unfamiliarity with the technology.

A trustworthy AI consultant should be willing to explain their actual costs.
"The voice agent platform costs me $75 per month per client. I spend about 8
hours setting it up. I charge $2,500 for setup and $250 per month. That
includes my ongoing maintenance and support."

That's honest. You can evaluate whether that's fair. When someone won't break
down their costs, they're usually hiding enormous margins.

### Red Flag #4: They Can't Explain It in Plain English

"We leverage proprietary multi-modal transformer architectures with
retrieval-augmented generation pipelines to optimize your customer engagement
funnel."

That sentence means almost nothing. And the person who said it may not even
know that.

Here's the plain English version: "We built a chatbot that reads your website
and FAQ so it can answer customer questions."

If someone can't explain what they're building for you in language you'd use
at dinner with your spouse, they either don't understand it themselves or
they're trying to intimidate you into not asking questions. Both are bad.

### Red Flag #5: They Promise "Set It and Forget It"

This is the biggest lie in AI right now. No AI system is set-and-forget.

AI makes mistakes. It will sometimes say wrong things to your customers. It
will occasionally do something bizarre that no one predicted. It needs
monitoring, updating, and adjustment — especially in the first few months.

Anyone who tells you they'll "install AI" and you'll never need to think about
it again is either lying or setting you up for a very bad surprise.

The honest version: "We'll set this up, test it, and monitor it closely for
the first 30 days. After that, we'll check in monthly and you'll have a
dashboard where you can see what it's doing. Things will go wrong sometimes —
we'll fix them quickly."

That's trustworthy. "Set it and forget it" is not.

## The Opportunity Is Real

I don't want this chapter to scare you away from AI entirely. That would be
just as wrong as the hype.

The truth is: AI genuinely can save most small businesses 5-10 hours per week
on tasks that are tedious, repetitive, and don't require your specific
expertise. For some businesses — especially those that answer a lot of phone
calls or produce a lot of written content — the savings can be much more.

The technology is real. The tools work. Many of them are free or cheap.
And you can learn to use most of them yourself, without hiring anyone.

That's what the rest of this book is about. Not how to hire an AI consultant.
How to become your own.
