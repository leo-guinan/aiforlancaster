# Video 1: Why I'm Giving Away My Entire Business Model
## The First YouTube Video — Full Script/Outline

**Length target:** 12-15 minutes (this one can run longer — it's the foundation)
**Format:** Talking head, direct to camera
**Thumbnail:** Dark background, white text: "MY ENTIRE AI BUSINESS MODEL" + Leo's face

---

## THE HOOK (first 30 seconds)

"I'm about to tell you exactly how I make money. Every detail. The pricing,
the margins, what it costs me, what I charge, and the part that makes most
consultants uncomfortable — my plan to make myself unnecessary to every client
I work with.

I'm doing this because I live in Lancaster, Ohio — population 40,000 — and
I'm starting an AI consulting business here on Main Street. Not in Silicon
Valley. Not for Fortune 500 companies. For the HVAC guy, the dentist, the
barber shop, the attorney down the street. And I think the best way to earn
trust is to show you everything before I ask for anything.

So here it is. The whole model. Take it, copy it, tell me I'm wrong. I don't
care. I'd rather be honest than clever."

---

## SECTION 1: THE BACKSTORY (2-3 minutes)

- I heard an episode of My First Million where Chris suggested going to your
  chamber of commerce and offering a free AI workshop
- I thought: that's good advice, but it's generic. What would it actually look
  like to DO it? In a specific town? With a specific plan?
- So instead of just thinking about it, I wrote a book. It's called AI for Main
  Street. It's a no-hype guide for small business owners. I'll link it below —
  the PDF is free.
- But the book is just the start. What I really want to talk about is the
  business model underneath it, because I think it's fundamentally different
  from what most AI consultants are doing.

---

## SECTION 2: WHAT EVERYONE ELSE IS DOING (2-3 minutes)

- The standard AI agency model: build a voice agent or chatbot, charge $2,500
  setup + $250/month recurring
- The actual costs: the platform runs maybe $50-100/month. So the consultant
  is making $150-200/month in pure margin per client. Forever.
- The incentive: keep the client dependent. Make the system complex enough
  that they can't run it without you. Never transfer the knowledge.
- This works great for the consultant. It's a terrible deal for the business
  owner. And in a small town, it's a ticking time bomb — because eventually
  the business owner figures it out, and now you're the guy who built a
  dependency instead of solving a problem.

"I don't want to be that guy. Not because I'm morally superior — I'm not.
Because I live here. I'll see these people at the grocery store. My kids will
go to school with their kids. Trust is the only currency that matters when
your market is 40,000 people."

---

## SECTION 3: MY MODEL (4-5 minutes — the core of the video)

### Phase 1: The Book
- I wrote AI for Main Street. 40,000 words. 12 chapters. Free PDF.
- The book teaches you to do most of this yourself. Seriously. If you read
  chapters 3 through 8, you probably don't need me.
- Why give that away? Because the 80% of businesses that CAN do it themselves
  should. The 20% that actually need help will trust me more because I didn't
  try to sell them something they could've done alone.

### Phase 2: The Audit Worksheet
- I pulled the audit exercise from the book into a standalone worksheet.
- Anyone can download it, walk through their business, find the opportunities.
- If you fill it out and send it to me, you get 50% off your onboarding fee.
- Why? Because someone who's already done the thinking is someone I can help
  faster. I'm not giving a discount. I'm pricing for reduced scope. It
  literally takes me less time when you've done the prep work.

### Phase 3: The Engagement
- Setup fee: $2,500 (or $1,250 if you did the audit). I build the system.
  Voice agent, automations, whatever you need.
- Here's where it gets different: I don't charge you $250/month forever.
  Instead, I train someone on your team.

### Phase 4: The Training — This Is the Key
- I pick someone on your team — usually the office manager, the front desk
  person, whoever is curious and not afraid of technology.
- I work alongside them. Show them how everything works. How to modify it.
  How to fix it when something breaks.
- After 30 days, they run it solo while I'm available for questions.
- Then I'm done. They own it.

"Think about what this does. Your employee just learned the most in-demand
skill in the economy. They're more valuable now — to you and to everyone
else. That's a retention tool. Pay them more. They're saving you the $250/month
you'd be paying me, plus they can actually improve the system because they
understand your business better than I ever will."

### The Referral Engine
- The person I trained goes to a Chamber of Commerce event.
- Someone asks how they got their voice agent working.
- They say: "Leo set it up and trained me to run it."
- That's worth more than 12 months of maintenance fees.
- In a small town, that's how businesses actually grow — not through ads,
  through word of mouth from people who were treated well.

---

## SECTION 4: THE NUMBERS — FULL TRANSPARENCY (2-3 minutes)

"Here are the actual numbers. I'm not going to hide them."

- Voice agent platform costs: $50-100/month per client (this is my cost)
- If I charged $250/month recurring, my margin would be $150-200
- Instead, I charge a flat training fee — probably $1,500-2,000 for the
  training phase
- So total engagement: $2,500 setup + $1,500 training = $4,000
  (or $1,250 + $1,500 = $2,750 if they did the audit)
- Their ongoing costs after I leave: $50-100/month for the platform.
  That's it. No consultant fee.

"My revenue per client is a little less than the recurring model over 12
months. But here's what I get instead: a referral source who actually
understands what I built, trust in a community where I plan to live for
the rest of my life, and the ability to sleep at night."

"My track record on predictions is 42%. I'm wrong more than I'm right.
I publish my misses. I don't pretend to have all the answers. What I can
promise is that I won't build a dependency and call it a service."

---

## SECTION 5: THE ASK (1 minute)

"Here's what I'd like you to do — and it's not 'buy my course.' I don't
have a course.

1. If you run a small business and you're curious about AI, download the
   free book. Link is below. Read chapter 1. If it's useful, keep reading.
   If it's not, no harm done.

2. If you want to try the audit, the worksheet is free too. Walk through
   your business. See what you find. If you send it to me, I'll give you
   50% off if we ever work together.

3. If you want to hear what a voice agent actually sounds like, call this
   number: [number]. It's a demo I built. It'll answer your questions about
   the book, about AI for small businesses, or it can walk you through the
   audit. It's an AI — it'll tell you that if you ask.

4. If you're a consultant and you want to steal this model — steal it.
   I mean that. The more people doing this honestly, the better it is for
   small businesses everywhere. I don't need a monopoly on helping people.

I'm Leo Guinan. I live in Lancaster, Ohio. I build AI systems and I think
Main Street can beat Silicon Valley if it has the right tools and the right
information.

Thanks for watching. I'll see you next week."

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- Record in one take if possible. Authenticity > polish.
- Sit at a desk or table. Clean background. Ring light.
- Look at the camera, not at notes. Use bullet points on a sticky
  note next to the lens if needed.
- The energy should be calm and direct. Not hyped. Not salesy.
  Like you're explaining this to a friend over coffee.
- Don't say "smash that like button." Don't say "don't forget to
  subscribe." Just make a good video. The rest follows.
