## 2. VIDEO FORMAT

### The Rules

- **Length**: 5-8 minutes. Hard ceiling at 8. If it's running long, you're covering too much. Split it.
- **Style**: Talking head. You, the camera, good lighting. That's it.
- **Camera**: Phone is fine. iPhone on a tripod. Horizontal (landscape). 1080p minimum.
- **Lighting**: One ring light directly in front of your face. This matters more than camera quality.
- **Background**: Clean wall, bookshelf, or office. Nothing distracting. Consistent across videos.
- **Audio**: Quiet room. No echo. If your room echoes, hang a blanket behind the camera.

### Video Structure (every single video)

```
[0:00 - 0:15]  HOOK — One sentence that makes them stay. State the problem or surprising fact.
[0:15 - 1:15]  PROBLEM — Why this matters to a business owner. Make it feel specific.
[1:15 - 5:15]  SOLUTION — The actual content. 3-4 key points. Practical, specific, actionable.
[5:15 - 5:45]  CTA — "I wrote a whole book on this. The PDF is free. Link in the description."
[5:45 - 6:00]  END — "If this was useful, subscribe. I post one of these a week."
```

### CTA Script (base version, vary slightly each video)

> "Everything I covered today is one chapter from my book, AI for Main Street. It's a no-hype guide for small business owners. The PDF is free — go to aiforlancaster.com or click the link in the description. If you're in Lancaster, I'll hand you a physical copy. Just email me."

## 4. PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

### Recording Setup (One-Time, 15 Minutes)

- Phone on a tripod (any $15 Amazon tripod works)
- Ring light in front of you, slightly above eye level
- Quiet room, door closed
- Clean background (same one every time — consistency builds brand)
- Phone in landscape mode, 1080p
- Frame yourself from chest up, eyes at the upper third of frame
- Test audio: record 10 seconds, play it back, make sure there's no echo or hum

### Pre-Recording (15 minutes)

- Write 5-6 bullet points on a sticky note next to the camera (NOT a full script)
- Practice the hook out loud once — just the first sentence
- Set a timer for 8 minutes visible behind the camera
- Hit record

### Recording (10-15 minutes)

- Say the hook. If you stumble, just pause and say it again. Don't stop recording.
- Talk through your bullets naturally
- If you lose your place, glance at the sticky note
- Do the CTA at the end
- Say "subscribe if this was useful" and stop
- You'll record 10-15 minutes of raw footage for a 6-8 minute final video

### Editing (30 minutes max)

- **Tool**: CapCut (free, phone or desktop) or iMovie
- Import the recording
- Trim dead air and long pauses
- Cut any false starts
- NO fancy transitions. NO music. NO b-roll. NO graphics.
- Add one title card at the start (optional — just text on screen with video title)
- Export

### Thumbnail (5 minutes)

- Open your Canva template (created once, duplicated forever)
- Change the keyword text
- Use the same headshot crop every time (take ONE good photo in your recording setup, use it for all 12)
- Export as PNG
- Done

- Title: the video title from the plan above

## 5. THE HONEST TAKE

### This channel will not blow up. That's fine.

Let's be real about what this is and isn't.

**What this is NOT:**
- A path to YouTube fame
- A way to make money from ads
- A content strategy that requires ongoing effort forever
- Something that needs to "perform"

**What this IS:**
- A library of 12 videos that each explain one useful concept
- A trust signal for anyone researching you or the book
- A permanent piece of search infrastructure
- Content you can link to in emails, hand out at chamber events, embed on your website

### Success Metrics (realistic)

- **50-200 views per video** = success for a local-focused channel
- **5-10 subscribers per month** = normal growth, don't sweat it
- **1 person emails you saying they found you through a video** = the whole thing paid for itself
- **Videos show up when someone Googles "AI small business Lancaster Ohio"** = the real goal

### Why This Works Even With Low Views

Every video is a permanent piece of trust infrastructure. When a potential client:
- Googles your name → they find 12 videos of you explaining things clearly
- Gets your book → they can watch you explain the same concepts on video
- Hears about you at a chamber event → they go home and watch a video before reaching out
- Gets a cold email from you → they check your YouTube and see you're legit

You're not competing with MrBeast. You're competing with the AI consultant who has zero public content and expects people to trust them based on a sales pitch.

### The Math That Matters

- 12 videos × 100 views each = 1,200 total views in year one
- If 1% of those viewers become leads = 12 leads
- If 25% of leads become clients at $2,500 = 3 clients = $7,500
- Total investment: ~24 hours of your time + $0 in cash
- That's $312/hour for your time. Not bad.
- And those videos keep working after you stop making them.

### Don't Chase the Algorithm. Chase Clarity.

- Don't look at analytics more than once a month
- Don't change your approach based on what "performs"
- Don't add music, intros, animations, or anything that increases production time
- Don't feel pressure to keep posting after the 12 are done
- Do make each video genuinely useful to someone who never reads the book
- Do speak plainly, like you're explaining something to a friend at Ale House 1890
- Do mention you're in Lancaster — local SEO is the whole game
