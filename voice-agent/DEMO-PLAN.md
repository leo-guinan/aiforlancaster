## 1. THE CONCEPT

A phone number that readers of "AI for Main Street" can call to experience
an AI voice agent firsthand. The agent is the living proof of Chapter 9.

Instead of just reading about what a voice agent sounds like, the reader
picks up their phone, dials a number, and has an actual conversation with
one. This is the equivalent of the "test call method" Leo describes in the
book — except you're test-calling Leo's own agent.

**What it does for readers:**
- Makes the abstract concrete. You stop imagining and start experiencing.
- Removes the fear factor. Talking to an AI for the first time in a low-
  stakes context (no money on the line, no appointment pressure) makes
  the technology feel approachable.
- Proves the book's claims. The book says voice agents sound natural, can
  book appointments, and handle questions. The demo line does all three.

**What it does for Leo's business:**
- Every caller is a warm lead. They're already reading the book.
- Demonstrates capability without a sales pitch. The demo IS the pitch.
- Provides call data on what small business owners actually care about
  (which becomes content for YouTube videos, book updates, and sales
  conversations).
- Creates a shareable moment. "Call this number and talk to an AI" is
  the kind of thing people tell other people about.

**The personality:**
- Friendly, competent, honest
- Slightly dry humor — Midwestern-friendly, not Silicon Valley slick
- Matches the tone of the book: no jargon, no hype, straight talk
- Never pretends to be human. If asked, says so immediately and
  cheerfully — then pivots to "and that's exactly what Chapter 9 is about"

Voice agents are like websites in 2005. Most businesses don't have one yet.

- Answers the phone 24/7
- Books appointments on the real calendar in real time
- Answers common questions (hours, pricing, services, location)
- Takes messages and routes urgent calls
- Handles multiple calls simultaneously
- Costs roughly $0.50-$2.00 per call in platform fees

Who benefits most: any business where the owner or employees are too busy

medical/dental offices, salons, auto repair, anyone where missing a call

- It will occasionally misunderstand people (accents, background noise)
- It can't handle truly complex or emotional conversations
- Some customers will hate it and want a human (always have a fallback)
- It needs to be kept current (hours, pricing, services change)

## 4. TECHNICAL SETUP

### Platform: Vapi

**Why Vapi over alternatives:**

- **Vapi vs Retell:** Vapi has better developer tooling, more flexible
  prompt configuration, native function-calling support (needed for
  calendar booking), and a more active community. Retell is solid but
  more opinionated about conversation flow. Vapi gives more control
  over the system prompt and tool integrations.
- **Vapi vs Bland:** Bland is enterprise-focused and more expensive.
  Overkill for a demo line.
- **Vapi vs building from scratch (Twilio + OpenAI Realtime API):**
  Building from scratch gives maximum control but adds 40+ hours of
  development time for handling interruptions, turn-taking, silence
  detection, and error recovery. Vapi handles all of this out of the
  box. Use Vapi now; consider custom-building later if the demo line
  becomes a core product.

Vapi pricing: ~$0.05/min platform fee + LLM costs + TTS costs + STT costs.
All-in per-minute cost is roughly $0.10-$0.15/min.

### Phone Number: Twilio (via Vapi)

Vapi integrates directly with Twilio. Get a local Ohio number if possible
(740 area code for Lancaster/Fairfield County) or a toll-free number.

- Twilio number cost: ~$1.50/month
- Per-minute Twilio cost: ~$0.0085/min (included in Vapi's pricing)
- Recommendation: Get a 740 number. It feels local. It matches the book's
  Lancaster, Ohio identity.

Alternative: Vapi also offers their own phone numbers, which simplifies
setup. Either works.

### Voice: ElevenLabs — "Adam" or "Charlie"

**Voice selection criteria:**
- Warm but professional
- Male voice (matches the book's author being male; callers may assume
  they're talking to "Leo's assistant" — a neutral, warm male voice
  avoids the gendered-assistant trope)
- American accent, not overly polished or newscaster-like
- Natural pacing, handles pauses well

**Top picks:**
1. **ElevenLabs "Adam"** — Warm, natural, conversational. Sounds like a
   real person having a real conversation. Not robotic. Slight warmth
   without being cheesy.
2. **ElevenLabs "Charlie"** — Slightly more casual, good energy. Works
   well for the friendly-but-competent vibe.
3. **Backup: Deepgram Aura "Orion"** — If ElevenLabs latency is an issue,
   Deepgram's Aura voices are faster (lower time-to-first-byte) and
   still sound good.

**Why not OpenAI TTS:** OpenAI's voices are good but tend to sound a bit
"smooth" — almost too polished. ElevenLabs voices have more texture and
natural variation, which feels more human in a phone conversation.

ElevenLabs cost via Vapi: ~$0.03-$0.05/min

### STT (Speech-to-Text): Deepgram Nova-2

- Best accuracy-to-speed ratio currently available
- Handles accents, background noise, and mumbling better than alternatives
- Cost via Vapi: ~$0.01-$0.02/min

### LLM: GPT-4o-mini

**Why GPT-4o-mini:**
- Fast enough for real-time conversation (low latency is critical)
- Smart enough to follow the system prompt, handle the audit
  conversation, and make booking tool calls
- Cheap: ~$0.15 per 1M input tokens, ~$0.60 per 1M output tokens
- For a 5-minute call, LLM cost is roughly $0.01-$0.03

**Why not GPT-4o or Claude:** Latency. In voice conversations, every
100ms of delay feels unnatural. GPT-4o-mini responds fast enough to
maintain conversational flow. GPT-4o and Claude are smarter but slower,
and the system prompt is detailed enough that 4o-mini handles it well.

**Consider upgrading to:** GPT-4o if callers report the agent feeling
"dumb" or missing nuance. But start with 4o-mini and monitor.

## 5. SETUP STEPS (In Order)

### Week 1: Foundation
1. Sign up for Vapi (vapi.ai)
2. Sign up for Twilio, get a 740 area code number
3. Connect Twilio number to Vapi
4. Set up Cal.com with Leo's availability for free assessments
5. Choose and test voice (ElevenLabs Adam via Vapi)

### Week 2: Build the Agent
1. Enter the system prompt (Section 3 above) into Vapi
2. Configure the booking function call
3. Build the webhook (Cal.com integration) — deploy to Vercel/Railway
4. Set up Twilio SMS for post-call follow-up
5. Configure call recording (with disclosure message)

### Week 3: Test Extensively
1. Leo calls the number 10+ times with different scenarios:
   - "What's this book about?"
   - "I run a plumbing company, can AI help me?"
   - "Are you a real person?"
   - "I want to book an assessment with Leo"
   - "Walk me through the audit"
   - "What are the red flags?"
   - Mumble, talk fast, interrupt, change the subject
   - Ask something completely off-topic
   - Pretend to be angry/frustrated
   - Ask for specific prices
2. Have 3-5 friends/family call and give honest feedback
3. Review every recording. Adjust the prompt based on issues.

### Week 4: Soft Launch
1. Add the number to the landing page (aiforlancaster.com)
2. Mention it in one YouTube video
3. Monitor calls daily for the first week
4. Fix issues as they come up (they will)

### Week 5+: Full Launch
1. Print the number in the book (Chapter 9)
2. Add to email signature, all marketing materials
3. Switch from daily to weekly monitoring
4. Monthly review of metrics and prompt adjustments

### Monthly Review

1. **Call volume trend** — Is it growing? Flat? Declining?
2. **Conversion rate** — What % of callers book an assessment?
3. **Top 10 questions** — These become:
   - YouTube video topics
   - Book update content
   - FAQ additions to the system prompt
4. **Failure modes** — Where did the agent get confused, give wrong info,
   or frustrate the caller? Fix these in the prompt.
5. **Average call duration trend** — Longer calls generally mean more
   engagement, but track whether they're productive long or confused long.
6. **Call-to-client pipeline** — Of callers who book assessments, how
   many become paying clients? This is the ultimate ROI metric.

### Quarterly Review

1. **Cost per lead** — Total monthly cost / number of assessment bookings
2. **Revenue attribution** — How much client revenue traces back to the
   demo line?
3. **System prompt evolution** — What's changed and why?
4. **Voice/model evaluation** — Are there better options now? (This space
   moves fast.)

### Call Recordings

- All calls recorded with caller disclosure at the start
- Store in Vapi (default) or export to cloud storage
- Review at least 10 random calls per week during month 1
- After month 1, review 5 per week
- After month 3, review flagged calls only (short calls, negative
  sentiment, booking failures)

## 9. LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS

1. **Call recording disclosure** — Required in two-party consent states
   (Ohio is one-party, but best practice is to disclose anyway). Add a
   brief message at the start: "This call may be recorded for quality
   improvement."

2. **AI disclosure** — The agent identifies itself as AI immediately.
   This is both ethical and increasingly required by regulations. The
   system prompt handles this.

3. **Data handling** — Caller information (name, phone, email) collected
   for booking purposes. Include a brief privacy note on the landing page
   about how caller data is used. Don't sell it. Don't share it.

4. **No unauthorized practice** — The agent doesn't give legal, medical,
   or financial advice. The system prompt includes this boundary.
