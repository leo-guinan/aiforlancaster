# Fork This Book

## AI for [Your Town] — An Open-Source Framework for Local AI Education

---

**One book can help one town. A network of books can change a country.**

---

## What This Is

This is an open-source book and website template. The original is called
*AI for Lancaster* — a practical guide to AI for small businesses in
Lancaster, Ohio. It was written by Leo Guinan because the people in his
town deserved honest, local, jargon-free AI education that wasn't trying
to sell them something.

Now you can make one for your town.

Fork the book. Replace "Lancaster" with your town's name. Add your local
resources, your Chamber of Commerce, your Main Street photo. Deploy it
as a free website. Join the network.

That's it. No permission needed. No fees. No platform lock-in. You own
your copy. The license is CC BY-SA 4.0 — share alike, give attribution,
and keep it open for the next person.


## The Thesis

**Trust is local.**

A business owner in Zanesville doesn't want AI advice from a Silicon
Valley influencer. They want it from someone who understands their town,
their customers, their constraints. Someone who has walked their Main
Street.

Big tech companies spend billions on AI marketing. Small towns get
nothing — or worse, they get hype that doesn't apply to them.

This project exists because AI education should be:

- **Local.** Written for your town, referencing your resources.
- **Free.** No paywalls, no upsells, no "premium tier."
- **Honest.** No hype. What works, what doesn't, what to watch out for.
- **Owned by the community.** Not by a platform. Not by a company.


## How the Network Works

This is not just a book template. It's a distributed network.

Every town that forks this book and deploys a site becomes a **node**.
Every node links to every other node. Every node links back to the
original. Every cross-link makes every site in the network rank higher
in search results.

Here's how it works:

```
1. You fork the book.
2. You localize it for your town.
3. You deploy it as a free website (aifor[yourtown].com).
4. You add your site to the network registry.
5. Every existing node adds a link to your site.
6. You add links to every existing node.
7. Everyone's search rankings go up.
8. More people in more towns find honest AI education.
```

**The network IS the product.**

One site ranking for "AI for small business in Lancaster" is nice. Fifty
sites ranking for fifty different towns, all cross-linked, all sharing
what content works — that's a movement. That's how you outrank the
venture-backed AI hype machine with nothing but good content and
collective action.

Read [NETWORK-PROTOCOL.md](NETWORK-PROTOCOL.md) for the full details on
how the SEO hivemind operates.


## What You Get

When you fork this project, you get:

- **A complete book** — 15 chapters covering AI fundamentals, practical
  tools, prompt engineering, and local resources. Most chapters are
  universal and work for any town without changes.

- **A website template** — A static site ready to deploy on Cloudflare
  Pages (free tier). Fast, no server costs, no maintenance headaches.

- **A localization guide** — Step-by-step instructions for making the
  book yours. See [TEMPLATE-GUIDE.md](TEMPLATE-GUIDE.md).

- **A network membership** — Cross-links, shared analytics, collective
  keyword research. Your site helps other sites. Other sites help yours.

- **A config file** — One YAML file where you set your town name, your
  domain, your local details. See [town-config.yaml](town-config.yaml).


## Step-by-Step: Fork This Book

### Prerequisites

- A GitHub account (free)
- A domain name: aifor[yourtown].com (~$12/year)
- A Cloudflare account (free tier is fine)
- 2-4 hours for localization
- Knowledge of your town (or willingness to learn)

### Steps

**1. Fork the repository.**

Click the "Fork" button on GitHub. You now have your own copy. Rename
the repo to match your town: `ai-for-[yourtown]`.

**2. Edit `town-config.yaml`.**

Open the config file and replace the Lancaster defaults with your town's
information. This is the single source of truth for your town name,
state, domain, Chamber of Commerce, and local resources.

```yaml
town: Zanesville
state: Ohio
domain: aiforzanesville.com
author: Your Name
author_email: you@example.com
chamber_of_commerce:
  name: Zanesville-Muskingum County Chamber of Commerce
  phone: 740.455.8282
local_resources: []
joined_network: 2025-07-15
```

**3. Localize the content.**

Follow [TEMPLATE-GUIDE.md](TEMPLATE-GUIDE.md) for detailed instructions
on which chapters to update and how. Most of the book works as-is. Only
Chapter 12 (local resources) needs significant local research.

**4. Take your Main Street photo.**

Go to your town's Main Street. Take a photo. Use it for the cover. This
is important — the photo signals "this is about YOUR town" to every
person who sees it. Stock photos won't cut it.

**5. Deploy your site.**

Push to GitHub. Connect to Cloudflare Pages. Point your domain. The
template includes everything you need. Total cost: $0/month + the
domain fee.

**6. Join the network.**

Open a pull request against the main network registry to add your site.
Include your `town-config.yaml`. Once merged, every node in the network
will add a link to your site, and you'll add links to theirs.

**7. Report monthly.**

Once a month, submit a simple markdown file with your traffic numbers
by page. No personal data — just aggregate page views. This helps the
whole network understand what content resonates. See
[NETWORK-PROTOCOL.md](NETWORK-PROTOCOL.md) for the reporting format.
