## 4. KDP SETTINGS RECOMMENDATIONS

### Pricing Strategy

**Kindle eBook: $9.99**
- Sweet spot for nonfiction business books in this category
- Qualifies for the 70% royalty tier ($2.99–$9.99)
- Estimated royalty: ~$6.99 per sale
- Competitive with similar titles; low enough to be an impulse buy for a business owner, high enough to signal "this is a real book, not a pamphlet"

**Paperback: $16.99**
- Standard for a ~157-page trade paperback in business/tech
- At 6x9 trim with ~157 pages, Amazon printing cost will be approximately $4.50–$5.50
- Estimated royalty: ~$4.00–$5.50 per sale (depending on final page count and marketplace)
- Business owners still buy paperbacks — especially ones they want to mark up, dog-ear, and hand to a partner or employee

**Hardcover (optional later): $24.99**
- Consider adding after launch if paperback performs well
- Good for bulk orders to Chambers of Commerce or business associations

### KDP Select: YES (at launch)

**Enroll for the first 90-day term.** Here's why:

1. **Kindle Unlimited exposure.** Many small business owners have KU subscriptions. Page reads add up and the algorithm rewards KU borrows with visibility.
2. **Promotional tools.** KDP Select gives you Kindle Countdown Deals and Free Book promotions. Use a Countdown Deal in weeks 3–4 post-launch to reignite momentum.
3. **You can exit after 90 days.** If you want to distribute wide (Apple Books, Kobo, etc.) later, you can choose not to renew. But for the launch window, the Amazon algorithm boost matters.
4. **Your audience is on Amazon.** Small business owners in Lancaster, Ohio are not browsing Kobo. Go where your readers are.

**Reassess after the first 90-day term.** If you're getting speaking gigs, Chamber events, or bulk orders, wide distribution may make more sense at that point.

### Publication Date Timing

**Recommended launch window: Early September 2025 or Early January 2026**

- **September:** Small business owners are back from summer, planning Q4 and next year's budget. "Should we invest in AI?" is a budget-season question. This is when they're most receptive.
- **January:** New year, new plans. Business owners making resolutions about efficiency, technology, growth. Strong alignment.
- **Avoid:** November–December (holiday noise drowns out nonfiction), June–August (summer slump for business books).

**If the book is ready sooner, don't wait.** A finished book sitting on a hard drive helps nobody. Ship it.

### Pre-Order Strategy

**Set up a Kindle pre-order 2–3 weeks before launch. No longer.**

- Long pre-order periods work for authors with massive email lists. For a first book launch, a short pre-order window is better.
- 2–3 weeks lets you: build anticipation with your local network, get commitments from Chamber contacts, line up any podcast or newsletter features.
- **All pre-order sales count toward Day 1 rankings.** This is the real reason to do it — you stack sales into a single day for the algorithm.
- **Upload the final manuscript before going live.** Don't use a placeholder. Amazon penalizes the experience and readers notice.

**Paperback: No pre-order.** Launch it same day as the Kindle. Paperback pre-orders on KDP are more hassle than they're worth for a first title.

### Additional Recommendations

- **A+ Content:** Once the book is published, set up A+ Content (enhanced brand content) on the Amazon listing. This lets you add comparison charts, additional images, and formatted text below the description. It's free and increases conversion.
- **ISBN:** Use the free Amazon-assigned ISBN for the paperback. You can always get your own later for wide distribution. Don't spend $125 on a Bowker ISBN for your first launch.
- **DRM:** Turn it OFF. DRM doesn't prevent piracy and it annoys legitimate customers. Business owners share books with partners and employees — let them.
- **Look Inside:** Make sure the first chapter is compelling. Most buyers read the Look Inside preview before purchasing. Chapter 1 needs to hook a skeptical business owner in the first two pages.
